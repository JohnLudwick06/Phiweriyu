#NPC

**House:** [[House Dunlaith]]
**Kingdom:** [[Fennock]]
**Role:** Lord of House Dunlaith
**Status:** Active

---

Lord Iwan Dunlaith is a tired, driven nobleman who has spent too many years arguing that the city can still be saved if people stop lying about the cost. He is no visionary, but he is stubborn enough to look like one to those who have given up.

### Personality

Iwan is blunt in the way of a man who used to be diplomatic and lost the patience for it. He interrupts, finishes arguments before they are made, and has a habit of stating the worst-case scenario before anyone else in the room has worked up to it. People find this either reassuring or infuriating, depending on whether they wanted to believe the comfortable version.

He does not have enemies so much as people who are tired of being right. He has been saying the same things about the capital for fifteen years and is mostly correct, which has made him difficult to argue with and easy to avoid.

He drinks more than he should, sleeps less, and has a bad back from years of walking wet embankments. He keeps working anyway.

### Appearance

Iwan is in his early sixties, gaunt where he was once heavy, with deep-set eyes and a permanent look of suppressed frustration. He dresses in house colors — grey and brown — that happen to look like working clothes. He carries a rolled engineering chart in his hand or tucked under his arm in almost every public setting, as though he might need to demonstrate something at any moment.

### Motivations

Iwan wants [[Fennock]]'s capital to survive another generation. He has no larger ambition than this. He would accept outside contracts, royal taxation, foreign engineers, and emergency debt if it meant the city did not simply disappear into the mire. He is not sentimental about the forms of governance required — he is sentimental about the city itself.

He is also increasingly aware that he may not live to see the outcome, and this has given him a quality that sometimes reads as urgency and sometimes as grief.

### Relationships

- **[[Eluned Dunlaith]]** — his daughter or niece (the house is not always clear about this). He relies on her for the political work he no longer has the temperament to do well, and has more faith in her than he tends to express.
- **Other Fennock houses** — Iwan is respected and avoided in roughly equal measure. When floodwater rises, people call him. When budgets are discussed, people look elsewhere.
- **Foreign engineers and outside contractors** — Iwan maintains relationships with skilled outsiders that other Fennock lords consider beneath them.
- **[[Lord Muiris Tiernan]]** — a correspondence born out of necessity and sustained by something closer to mutual recognition. Iwan needs [[Thiadery]] timber at predictable prices and reliable intervals; Muiris needs buyers who do not pretend the cost of food security is negotiable. They have been dealing directly, bypassing several layers of merchant and factor, for the better part of a decade. Neither would call it friendship. Both would admit that the other is one of the few people they speak to without adjusting what they say first. Iwan once told Muiris that [[Fennock]] would be underwater in a generation if things continued as they were. Muiris replied that he already knew, and that the timber would still be available as long as the coin was. Iwan found this more comforting than he expected.
### Possible Adventure Hooks

- Iwan may hire the party to escort engineers through hostile marsh territory or retrieve stolen construction materials.
- He may need a political obstacle removed before a crucial embankment project can begin.
- The party may be the first people in years who actually listen to what he is saying — which may make them useful to him in ways neither party anticipated.
