#NPC

**House:** [[House Tiernan]]
**Kingdom:** [[Thiadery]]
**Role:** Lord of House Tiernan
**Status:** Active

---

Lord Muiris Tiernan is a patient and unsentimental landholder who treats shortages, tariffs, and border incidents as problems of timing and pressure. He is rarely the loudest man in a room, but he is often the one everyone must reckon with once provisions become scarce.

### Personality

Muiris has a quality that is sometimes mistaken for passivity: he waits. He waits for arguments to exhaust themselves. He waits for his rivals to commit to positions that provisions will shortly make untenable. He waits for the moment when everyone else in the room is hungry enough to be reasonable. This is not patience as a virtue — it is patience as a weapon, and he has been wielding it for thirty years.

He is calm in the way of someone who genuinely does not need the conversation to go one way or the other right now. He will get what he needs eventually. This makes him very difficult to pressure and very easy to underestimate until harvest negotiations begin.

He is not a warm man, but he is a fair one in the specific sense that he holds himself to the same terms he holds others. He has a reputation for honoring contracts exactly — no more, no less — which is a kind of honesty that leaves people feeling watched.

### Appearance

Muiris is in his late fifties, of average height, with the deliberate stillness of someone who moves purposefully or not at all. His clothes are expensive and conservative, in the greys and deep greens of fertile land. He does not gesture when he speaks. He listens with his hands folded.

### Motivations

Muiris wants [[Thiadery]] to stop giving away its agricultural leverage through internal disorganization. He has watched [[Nerath]] exploit Thiadery's grain surpluses for years by playing its nobles against each other, and he is methodically working to prevent that — not through sentiment or patriotism but because he would prefer to set the price himself.

He is also aware that his daughter [[Ailis Tiernan]] is better at the broader coalition work than he is, and he is slowly transferring responsibility to her without formally saying so.

### Relationships

- **[[Ailis Tiernan]]** — his eldest daughter. He trusts her analysis over his own in most political matters and has arranged for her to be present at nearly every significant negotiation for the past five years. He has not told her why.
- **[[Lady Rhiannon Brennath]]** — a natural ally and a genuine peer relationship. They disagree about timber versus grain as a primary lever, but they understand each other's logic.
- **Thiadery grain merchants and mill holders** — Muiris maintains these relationships with the same care he gives noble alliances. They are, to him, equally real.
-  **[[Lord Iwan Dunlaith]]** — a direct buyer and one of the few Fennocki nobles Muiris considers worth the correspondence. Iwan does not perform optimism about [[Fennock]]'s situation, which Muiris respects, and he pays on time, which Muiris respects more. Their arrangement cuts out the usual intermediary layers, which suits both of them and irritates several merchant houses that would prefer to be in the middle. Muiris is aware that if the capital sinks badly enough Iwan's ability to pay will disappear along with everything else, and he has thought carefully about at what point that risk changes the terms. He has not yet reached that point. He is watching.

### Possible Adventure Hooks

- Muiris may hire the party to investigate disruptions to a grain convoy that he suspects were not random.
- He may be the key to an alliance the party needs — and his price may be something entirely practical and unglamorous.
- The party may learn that a shortage they are dealing with traces back to a decision Muiris made months earlier, for reasons that are entirely coherent and not personally malicious.
