#NPC

**Clan:** [[Clan Eirsvold]]
**Kingdom:** [[Larnack]] / [[Fuarnarth]] border
**Role:** Thane of Clan Eirsvold; warlord of the hill-roads
**Status:** Active

---

Thane Hrold Eirsvold is a scarred veteran who smells of smoke and forge oil and judges people as bluntly as he judges iron. He values strength, discipline, and readiness, and sees politics largely through the lens of whether a kingdom is prepared to survive the next crisis.

### Personality

Hrold operates on a single guiding principle: things either work or they don't, and the difference can be tested. He applies this to metal, to fortifications, to people, and to political arrangements. He has no patience for arguments that cannot be falsified by a hard winter or an enemy column, and he says so, usually while someone is still making the argument.

He is not stupid — he understands that politics exists and that it must be navigated — but he treats it as an unfortunate necessity rather than an interesting domain. He delegates the courtly work to others where he can and endures it where he can't.

He respects competence across factional lines. If a Thiadery engineer or a Fennock marsh-worker demonstrates genuine skill, Hrold will say so, which sometimes confuses people who expected him to be more tribal. He is tribal about quality, not origin.

He is also, underneath the severity, loyal in a way that is almost old-fashioned. People who have proven themselves to him stay proven. He does not revise his regard downward based on politics.

### Appearance

Hrold is in his early sixties, built like someone who has spent a lifetime in armor, with a wide jaw, a nose broken more than once, and scars on his hands, neck, and the left side of his face that he has never explained to anyone's satisfaction. He smells of the forge because he visits it regularly. His gear is functional and very well-maintained.

### Motivations

Hrold wants [[Larnack]] to take its martial capacity seriously. He believes that the kingdom's reliance on trade relationships and diplomatic positioning has left it structurally vulnerable, and he is working to bind it more tightly to [[Fuarnarth]]'s engineering and metallurgical strength. He sees [[Clan Eirsvold]]'s control of the hill-roads as the natural bridge for this relationship.

He also wants the fortifications along those roads upgraded before someone forces the issue, and he is frustrated by how long it is taking to get anyone to fund it properly.

### Relationships

- **[[Kara Coal-Singer]]** — his Fuarnarth counterpart and, in his private assessment, one of the most capable people he has worked with. He defers to her on engineering questions entirely and makes no pretense of expertise he doesn't have, which she respects.
- **[[Jarl Sten Skalligr]]** — a strong working relationship based on shared values. Hrold trusts Sten's assessment of what Fuarnarth can and cannot provide.
- **[[Lord Cadan Brannoc]]** — a complicated alliance. Hrold respects Cadan's military history and finds his Nerathi nostalgia irrelevant. They cooperate on border defense when their interests align.

### Possible Adventure Hooks

- Hrold may hire the party to scout a section of the hill-roads where something has been moving that his patrols haven't been able to identify.
- He may need outside intermediaries to carry a proposal to a [[Fuarnarth]] faction he cannot approach directly without it looking like a military overture.
- The party may encounter Hrold investigating a fortification failure that looks like it was deliberately caused.
