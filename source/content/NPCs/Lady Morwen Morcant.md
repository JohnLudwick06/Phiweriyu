#NPC

**House:** [[House Morcant]]
**Kingdom:** [[Fennock]]
**Role:** Lady of House Morcant
**Status:** Active

---

Lady Morwen Morcant is unsentimental, sharp-eyed, and difficult to surprise. She speaks with the calm certainty of someone who has watched grand promises disappear beneath black water more than once.

### Personality

Morwen does not get angry visibly. She does not celebrate visibly. She processes what is in front of her and responds to its actual content rather than its presentation, which makes her very hard to flatter and very easy to bore. She is not cold — she is simply done being surprised by human behavior.

She has a talent for silence that unnerves people. When she does not answer immediately, it is rarely because she is thinking — it is usually because she is deciding how much of what she has already thought to share.

She has an excellent memory for who helped her and who didn't, and she keeps those ledgers private and current.

### Appearance

Morwen is in her late forties, of middle height and compact build, with weathered skin and grey beginning at her temples. She dresses practically but with deliberate quality — everything she wears is built to last and chosen to be underestimated. Her expression at rest reads as neutral and is often mistaken for boredom.

### Motivations

Morwen wants House Morcant to remain indispensable through the ongoing collapse of [[Fennock]]'s capital. She understands that disaster is her industry. She does not cause the floods, the sinking foundations, or the failing causeways — but she intends to be the person people call when those things happen, and she intends to be paid fairly for it.

She is not without principle: she will not let her own communities drown for profit, and she maintains the marsh passages under her control at genuine expense. But she has little patience for nobles who want the benefits of her knowledge without the cost of actual partnership.

### Relationships

- **[[Cadoc Morcant]]** — her son or a close cousin, depending on the conversation. She relies on him for the marsh-level operations and trusts his information. She does not entirely trust his judgment about when to stop.
- **[[Eluned Dunlaith]]** — a genuine mutual wariness. Morwen respects Eluned's intelligence and suspects her of playing a longer game than she admits to. They cooperate when their interests align, which is more often than either acknowledges publicly.
- **Marsh communities** — Morwen has genuine relationships in the wetlands that most great houses have never bothered to cultivate. This is one of her real advantages.

### Possible Adventure Hooks

- Morwen may offer passage through a difficult marsh route — for a price that includes a task, not just coin.
- She may need a salvage operation handled discreetly, either because the cargo is sensitive or because another house is watching the route.
- The party may encounter Morwen dealing with someone who tried to take advantage of one of her people, and have to decide which side to take.
