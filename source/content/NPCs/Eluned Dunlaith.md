#NPC

**House:** [[House Dunlaith]]
**Kingdom:** [[Fennock]]
**Role:** Political representative of House Dunlaith
**Status:** Active

---

Eluned Dunlaith is younger, more charismatic, and far better at presenting sacrifice as a shared cause rather than an accounting problem. She may be one of the few Fennocki nobles capable of inspiring loyalty beyond her immediate district.

### Personality

Eluned has a gift for making people feel that what is being asked of them matters — that they are part of something real rather than merely recipients of a policy. This is not manipulation in the crude sense; she largely believes what she says. But she is also aware that genuine conviction is the most persuasive tool available, and she has learned to deploy it deliberately.

She is warmer in manner than [[Lord Iwan Dunlaith]] and more patient with the inefficiencies of coalition-building. She understands that some arguments must be won slowly, and she is willing to spend years on a foundation before she asks anyone to build on it.

She is not naive. She knows that [[Fennock]] may not survive regardless of what anyone does, and she has begun thinking about what the house's legacy should be if the worst comes — what structures, records, or communities she can preserve even if the city itself is lost.

### Appearance

Eluned is in her early thirties, with a deliberately cultivated warmth in how she presents herself: accessible clothing, direct eye contact, a tendency to reach out and touch an arm when making a point. She is tall and moves with confidence. She wears house colors but in lighter, more approachable shades than Iwan.

### Motivations

Eluned wants to build a constituency large enough to actually fund and execute the capital's survival — not just argue for it. She is working on relationships with lesser marsh lords, guild leaders, and the communities of workers who actually maintain the embankments. She believes [[Lord Iwan Dunlaith]] is right about the problem and wrong about how to get anyone to help solve it.

She is also thinking longer than he is. If [[Fennock]] cannot save the capital, it may be able to relocate its center of gravity before the worst occurs — and she wants House Dunlaith positioned to lead that transition if it comes.

### Relationships

- **[[Lord Iwan Dunlaith]]** — the older lord she works beside. She protects his reputation, softens his edges in public, and privately worries that his exhaustion is becoming visible. She would not replace him before his time, but she is prepared.
- **Lesser marsh lords and guild officials** — the relationships she is building that no one else in the great houses has bothered with.
- **[[Lady Morwen Morcant]]** — a complicated relationship. Eluned recognizes Morwen's intelligence and distrusts her motives. They are careful with each other.
- **[[Ailis Tiernan]]** — a correspondence that began as a timber procurement inquiry and has become something more strategic. Eluned understands that [[Fennock]]'s dependency on [[Thiadery]] wood is a vulnerability, but also a relationship — and that Ailis is one of the few people on the other end of it who thinks the same way. They have not met in person.

### Possible Adventure Hooks

- Eluned may hire the party to represent Dunlaith interests at a gathering of minor lords she cannot attend without it looking political.
- She may ask for help locating something — a resource, a person, a piece of information — that could shift a specific noble's position on the capital's funding.
- The party may become caught between Eluned's long-term plans and someone else's short-term interests.
