#NPC

**House:** [[House Aedraig]]
**Kingdom:** [[Nerath]]
**Role:** Temple Administrator; younger son of House Aedraig
**Status:** Active

---

Brother Malrec Aedraig is a younger son who took vows not out of spiritual conviction but as a calculated path into the heart of Nerath's sacred and archival institutions. He is deeply learned and more ambitious than his vows suggest. He serves as a bridge between noble interests and sacred institutions, quietly shaping which histories are remembered, emphasized, or allowed to fade.

### Personality

Malrec is affable, curious, and genuinely interested in ideas — which makes him more dangerous than a simple schemer. He can hold a real theological debate and enjoy it, argue over competing interpretations of old texts with evident enthusiasm, and still walk away having directed the conversation exactly where he intended. His warmth reads as sincerity because it partly is.

He is careful never to appear overtly political. He presents every manipulation as scholarship, duty, or administrative necessity. If pressed, he will invoke the needs of the institution, not his house, even in a room full of people who know better.

### Appearance

Malrec is in his mid-thirties, with a scholar's slight build and an unhurried way of moving that makes him seem younger than he is. He wears the plain robes of his order — always well-kept, never ostentatious. His hands are ink-stained. His eyes are bright and attentive in a way that makes people feel watched even when he appears relaxed.

### Motivations

Malrec wants to make House Aedraig's influence structural rather than political — embedded in the actual recordkeeping, liturgical calendar, and legal memory of Nerath itself. He is working to ensure that key appointments, archival interpretations, and sacred rulings favor Aedraig positions without ever appearing to do so.

On a personal level, he is genuinely ambitious for knowledge and resents that his birth order forced him to seek influence through ceremony rather than direct inheritance. He would be dangerous with more resources. He may already have more than anyone suspects.

### Relationships

- **[[Lady Eithne Aedraig]]** — his older sister and the person who made his position possible. He is loyal to her but occasionally chafes at her conservatism. She thinks in generations; he thinks in opportunities.
- **Temple and archival staff** — Malrec has cultivated a network of clerks, junior priests, and scribes who owe him small favors and large silence.
- **[[Crown of Nerath]]** — Malrec is careful to remain theologically useful to the crown, which insulates him from political scrutiny.
- **[[Yrsa Coal-Blood]]** — a correspondence that began when Yrsa needed access to old Nerathi survey records of the southern coastline, which Malrec's archival position made possible. He provided them, asked nothing immediately, and waited. She noticed the wait. They have since exchanged four letters, each one technically about something innocuous, each one carrying more than it admits to. Malrec finds her useful because she moves through [[Fuarnarth]]'s interior unsupervised and notices things. She finds him useful for reasons she has not fully articulated yet, which is itself something she finds worth examining.

### Possible Adventure Hooks

- The party may need records that have been officially forgotten — Malrec may know where they are, and want something in return.
- He may approach the party to quietly acquire a text, relic, or witness that he cannot be seen pursuing directly.
- A junior archivist may seek the party's help exposing what Malrec has been selectively preserving or destroying.
