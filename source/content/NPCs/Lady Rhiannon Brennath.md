#NPC

**House:** [[House Brennath]]
**Kingdom:** [[Thiadery]]
**Role:** Lady of House Brennath
**Status:** Active

---

Lady Rhiannon Brennath is brisk, dry-witted, and more comfortable in a riding cloak than in embroidered silk. She thinks in terms of labor, weather, and bottlenecks, and she has little patience for any courtier who speaks as though wood simply appears where needed.

### Personality

Rhiannon is practical to a fault and wears it deliberately. She knows that her bluntness about costs, logistics, and resource limits makes courtiers uncomfortable, and she has decided this is their problem rather than hers. She is not rude — she is efficient — and there is a meaningful difference, though not everyone she deals with sees it.

She has a mordant sense of humor that emerges mostly around people she trusts. She makes jokes about drowning, collapse, and contracts that fall through — the kind of humor that develops when most of your work involves preventing disasters that are always one hard winter away.

### Appearance

Rhiannon is in her forties, weathered and lean, with sun-darkened skin and several small scars on her hands from years of work in the forests. Her hair is kept short or tied close. She dresses practically: heavy cloth, quality boots, functional belts. At court she makes minor concessions to ceremony without enthusiasm.

### Motivations

Rhiannon wants House Brennath's timber routes to remain profitable and operationally secure regardless of what happens between [[Nerath]] and [[Thiadery]]. She is willing to sell to either side in a crisis — she has done it before — and she would rather not be forced to choose one kingdom as a patron, because patrons expect loyalty and she prefers contracts.

She is increasingly concerned that [[Fennock]]'s structural collapse is becoming a problem her house can profit from in ways that will look terrible if anyone documents them carefully.

### Relationships

- **[[Edern Brennath]]** — her younger brother. She relies on him to smooth relationships she sometimes damages with honesty. She thinks he is too comfortable with ambiguity but concedes that it serves the house.
- **[[House Tiernan]]** — a natural ally in Thiadery's internal politics, though Rhiannon finds Lord Muiris's patience somewhat theatrical.
- **Barge guild factors** — Rhiannon maintains these relationships directly and personally, considering them more important than most noble connections.

### Possible Adventure Hooks

- The party may need to negotiate passage of goods through Brennath-controlled forest routes.
- A shipment meant for [[Fennock]] has gone missing — or a rival is trying to intercept it.
- Rhiannon may hire the party to investigate who has been cutting timber in her managed forests without authorization.
