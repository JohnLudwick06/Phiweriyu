#NPC

**House:** [[House Rhuvann]]
**Kingdom:** [[Nerath]]
**Role:** Commander of House Rhuvann patrol vessels
**Status:** Active

---

Captain Siofra Rhuvann commands several of the house's patrol vessels and has earned a reputation for being both disciplined and unforgiving. She treats smuggling, espionage, and diplomatic ambiguity as the same problem viewed from different distances.

### Personality

Siofra is precise, controlled, and absolutely certain of the standards she holds herself and her crews to. She is not harsh for its own sake — her discipline comes from a genuine belief that lax enforcement causes deaths. She has seen what happens when coastal authority is ambiguous or inconsistently applied, and she does not intend to let that happen in her waters.

She has her father's directness but a cooler affect. Where [[Lord Taran Rhuvann]] can be warm with those he trusts, Siofra rarely allows herself warmth on the job. She is professional to the edge of coldness with most people, and privately wonders sometimes if she has let the job narrow her in ways she should address.

She is, behind that controlled exterior, highly observant about people — their motivations, their tells, their loyalties. She simply rarely shares those observations, because most conversations do not require it.

### Appearance

Siofra is in her early thirties, lean and efficient in everything including how she takes up space. She keeps her dark hair close-cropped for practicality. Her uniform is immaculate and always operational — she could go on deck immediately from any meeting. She wears no jewelry on duty and considers that a statement.

### Motivations

Siofra wants to prove that rigorous, lawful maritime enforcement is both possible and strategically superior to the aggressive ambiguity that some Nerathi factions prefer. She believes the island dispute can be held through law and presence rather than provocation, and she is quietly opposed to the faction within [[House Rhuvann]] that sees escalation as an opportunity.

She also wants the captaincy of a full patrol squadron before she is forty, which requires her to remain both effective and politically readable enough not to threaten anyone above her.

### Relationships

- **[[Lord Taran Rhuvann]]** — her father. She knows he trusts her more than almost anyone. She finds this both motivating and a source of private pressure, because she does not want his regard to be a factor in her promotion.
- **Her crews** — she is not beloved, but she is trusted, which she considers more important. The crews who have served with her longest request to stay on her ships.
- **Merchants and smugglers in the coastal zone** — Siofra has a detailed, current knowledge of who is operating where and under what arrangements. She knows more than she acts on, by policy, which sometimes makes her uncomfortable.
- **[[Brigid Faelkir]]** — a complicated professional awareness. Brigid moves goods through border channels that occasionally touch Nerathi coastal waters, and Siofra knows it. She has not acted on it, partly because Brigid has never given her a clean jurisdictional excuse, and partly because Brigid has twice passed her information about third parties that proved accurate. Siofra has not decided what this arrangement is yet.
- **[[Hakon Valdyr]]** — a professional acquaintance who operates just inside the boundaries of what Siofra can formally object to, and knows it. Hakon's foreign correspondence network occasionally moves goods through Nerathi coastal approaches, always with the right paperwork, always slightly more than the paperwork suggests. Siofra has boarded two Valdyr vessels. Both inspections were clean. She does not think this means the vessels were clean.

### Possible Adventure Hooks

- Siofra may grant the party sea passage in exchange for work — information gathering, a retrieved manifest, a witness to something she cannot board and search for officially.
- She may approach the party because they have been seen with someone she is watching and she wants to understand the connection.
- She may need unofficial help with something that falls outside her mandate — the kind of thing that exists specifically because some problems cannot be solved by a commissioned officer with a logged ship.
