#NPC

**Hold:** [[Faelkir Hold]]
**Kingdom:** [[Fennock]] / [[Larnack]] border
**Role:** Illegitimate daughter of Jarl Torfi Faelkir; smuggler and courier
**Status:** Active

---

Brigid Faelkir moves easily between the worlds of smugglers and courtiers. Restless, observant, and hard to pin down, she represents the younger generation most likely to reject the hypocrisies of the existing order.

### Personality

Brigid is quick where her father is slow, direct where he is oblique, and genuinely uncomfortable with the kind of power that operates entirely through quiet dependency. She understands it — she grew up watching it — and she can use it when she has to. She just finds it exhausting in a way her father never did.

She has an instinct for people that is less analytical than his and more immediate. She reads a room quickly, decides what she thinks of everyone in it, and then starts figuring out who might be useful and who might be fun. These categories overlap more than most people expect.

She is not reckless, despite appearances. The restlessness is genuine, but she is also very good at knowing when to stay still. She has talked her way out of situations that should have ended worse, and she has done it calmly enough that people weren't sure afterward whether they'd been outmaneuvered.

### Appearance

Brigid is in her late twenties, lean and quick-moving, with her father's dark coloring and none of his stillness. She dresses for function and transition — clothes that work in a tavern, a hold-house, and a marsh crossing without requiring a change. She carries a short knife she does not advertise and a reputation she does not fully deserve in either direction.

### Motivations

Brigid wants to understand what the border economy actually looks like from the inside before she decides what to do about it. She has been building her own network — separate from her father's, overlapping in ways he doesn't fully track — and she is not sure yet whether she will use it to inherit his position, undermine it, or leave.

She is also genuinely curious about [[Fennock]]'s broader instability in a way that is starting to feel less like observation and more like responsibility. She has seen what the flooding does to the marsh communities that her father lends to, and she is not sure the lending helps as much as it claims to.

### Relationships

- **[[Jarl Torfi Faelkir]]** — her father. She is loyal to him in the specific way of someone who was not entirely acknowledged and decided to earn regard anyway. She knows he uses her. She uses the access this provides.
- **Smugglers and marsh guides** — her primary network. She knows routes, people, and informal arrangements across the border zone that official records don't contain.
- **[[Cadoc Morcant]]** — a professional acquaintance whose networks border hers in ways that are occasionally collaborative and occasionally competitive. They have a working understanding.
- **[[Captain Siofra Rhuvann]]** — the [[Nerath]] patrol captain who knows Brigid operates in waters she technically oversees and has chosen not to act on it yet. Brigid finds this more unsettling than being chased would be. She has passed Siofra information twice, which she tells herself was pragmatic and suspects was something else. She does not entirely know what she is building with this.

### Possible Adventure Hooks

- Brigid may be exactly who the party needs to move something across the border without official notice.
- She may approach the party because they have access to a person or place she cannot reach through her own channels.
- The party may end up with information about the hold's debt arrangements that both Brigid and her father want handled differently, forcing a choice.
