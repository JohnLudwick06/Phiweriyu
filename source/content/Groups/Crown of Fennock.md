

The crown of [[Fennock]] rules under conditions that would break more comfortable kingdoms. Its authority is burdened by a capital that is literally sinking, by dependence on imported materials, and by marsh lords whose local control often matters more day to day than royal declarations. In such a realm, kingship is less a matter of splendor than of constant crisis management.

Even so, the crown remains symbolically vital. If the center is abandoned, many fear the kingdom will cease to think of itself as one realm at all. For that reason, every investment in repairs, embankments, or imported timber is also an investment in political unity, however temporary or fragile.
