#House

House Dunlaith is one of the few [[Fennock|Fennocki]] houses that still tries to think in openly royal terms rather than purely local survival. Its holdings include older sections of the capital and several of the engineered embankments that keep high-status districts from vanishing into the mire. For that reason, the house’s fortunes rise and fall with the city itself.

Dunlaith argues for large-scale coordination, outside contracts, and the painful taxation needed to preserve the capital. This wins it little affection among smaller marsh lords, who suspect that every kingdom-wide effort somehow ends with Dunlaith owning more stable ground. Nevertheless, when collapse threatens, even rivals must admit that the house has experience in organizing desperate civic labor.

### [[Lord Iwan Dunlaith]]

Lord Iwan Dunlaith is a tired, driven nobleman who has spent too many years arguing that the city can still be saved if people stop lying about the cost. He is no visionary, but he is stubborn enough to look like one to those who have given up.

### [[Eluned Dunlaith]]

Eluned Dunlaith is younger, more charismatic, and far better at presenting sacrifice as a shared cause rather than an accounting problem. She may be one of the few Fennocki nobles capable of inspiring loyalty beyond her immediate district.
