

The nobility of [[Thiadery]] is broad, local, and often only loosely coordinated. Unlike the more tightly symbolic court culture of [[Nerath]] or the trade-centered maneuvering of [[Larnack]], Thiadery’s power is distributed across productive regions. Lords rise through grain, timber, pasture, river access, and the loyalties of their local dependents. This gives the kingdom depth and resilience, but it also makes unified action slow and politically expensive.

Many Thiaderyn nobles believe the realm’s greatest weakness is not poverty or poor land, but division. The feud with [[Nerath]] over the seized island has sharpened that view, especially after [[Fuarnarth]] shifted its food purchases away from Thiadery. Some lords now argue that every delay in response invites another humiliation.

Notable houses include [[House Tiernan]], whose grain power shapes questions of food security and bargaining strength, and [[House Brennath]], whose timber routes have become increasingly important because of [[Fennock]]’s structural crisis. Both benefit from peace in theory, yet both are positioned to profit from tension, shortages, and the need for decisive action.
