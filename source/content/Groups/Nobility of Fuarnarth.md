

The nobility of [[Fuarnarth]] is inseparable from the kingdom’s infrastructure. In a land warmed and sustained by volcanic tunnels, authority belongs not only to those with banners and bloodlines but to those who control roads beneath the earth, forge output, heated chambers, and the dangerous systems that keep settlements alive. This creates a political culture that values competence unusually highly, even when old lineage still matters.

Fuarnarthi houses tend to divide between those oriented toward the deep interior and those focused on ports, surface keeps, and foreign exchange. Both know that survival in the south requires discipline. What they disagree on is how much the kingdom should commit itself to broader continental entanglements.

Notable houses include [[House Skalligr]], which draws its power from tunnel authority and subterranean movement, and [[House Valdyr]], whose commercial and coastal networks shape much of Fuarnarth’s external policy. The latter was especially influential in the kingdom’s decision to begin buying food from [[Nerath]] instead of [[Thiadery]], proving that trade decisions in Fuarnarth can have consequences far beyond the snow line.
