

The crown of [[Thiadery]] sits above a kingdom that is wealthy, productive, and difficult to command in a unified way. Royal authority depends as much on negotiation as decree, because so much local power rests in the hands of grain lords, timber houses, river stewards, and provincial magnates. A strong king can coordinate Thiadery. A weak one merely presides over it.

This makes the crown’s current situation especially dangerous. The feud with [[Nerath]] has created pressure for a firmer response, but every serious action requires cooperation from nobles who expect compensation, advantage, or concessions in return. The king must therefore balance dignity with patience, knowing that too little action looks weak and too much may reveal how fragmented the realm truly is.
