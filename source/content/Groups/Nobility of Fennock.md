# Nobility of Fennock

The nobility of [[Fennock]] is shaped above all by water, decay, salvage, and the politics of survival. Rank matters, but geography matters more. A lord who controls a viable crossing, a line of pilings, or a stretch of navigable marsh may wield more real power than one with a finer pedigree and less usable ground. As a result, Fennocki nobility often appears pragmatic, suspicious, and intensely local.

Some houses, such as [[House Morcant]], thrive because they know how to work the swamp as it is. Others, such as [[House Dunlaith]], tie their fortunes to saving the capital and preserving some larger royal order. Both approaches are responses to the same crisis: a kingdom whose center is literally sinking and whose future depends on costly repairs, imported timber, and whoever can turn emergency into authority.

Figures like [[Faelkir Hold]] matter enormously in this environment, because the routes into and out of Fennock are as politically significant as any throne room. The kingdom cannot isolate itself, and every dependency gives another hand leverage over its fate.
