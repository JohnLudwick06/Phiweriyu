
Across [[Phiweriyu]], noble power has evolved in different directions since the fall of the [[Old Empire]]. In [[Nerath]], authority leans on age, ritual, and institutions of memory. In [[Thiadery]], it rests on agricultural output, forests, and local lordship. In [[Fennock]], it is shaped by control of crossings, salvage, and the struggle against environmental collapse. In [[Fuarnarth]], noble legitimacy depends heavily on competence, infrastructure, and command of dangerous but life-giving subterranean systems. In [[Larnack]], the nobility is deeply entangled with trade, provinces, and foreign patronage.

These differences matter because the kingdoms remain officially at peace while their noble classes increasingly see tension as opportunity. Kings attempt to preserve order, but ambitious houses are often willing to test borders, manipulate supply, exploit crisis, or deepen dependency if it improves their standing. The current age of Phiweriyu is therefore one in which aristocratic competition is gradually eroding royal restraint.
