#House
House Brennath controls some of [[Thiadery]]’s richest managed forests and lumber routes, giving it outsized influence in a kingdom whose timber is needed far beyond its own borders. The house profits heavily from river drives, woodland levies, and the contracts that keep distant settlements standing, especially in crisis years. It has grown even more important as [[Fennock]] has increased its demand for structural timber to keep its sinking capital from collapsing.

Brennath presents itself as a practical house of woodwardens, surveyors, and foresters, closer to axe and tally than to jeweled courtly display. Yet its influence is deeply political. Whoever controls the timber routes can delay repairs, raise prices, reward allies, or quietly punish rivals. In times of strain, House Brennath can shape diplomacy simply by deciding which shipments arrive on time.

### [[Lady Rhiannon Brennath]]

Lady Rhiannon Brennath is brisk, dry-witted, and more comfortable in a riding cloak than in embroidered silk. She thinks in terms of labor, weather, and bottlenecks, and she has little patience for any courtier who speaks as though wood simply appears where needed.

### [[Edern Brennath]]

Edern Brennath oversees the downstream movement of timber and keeps the house’s relationships with barge guilds and caravan factors. He is more polished than his sister but no less calculating, and he understands exactly how much politics can be hidden inside a shipping schedule.
