#Kingdom
Fennock is the swamp kingdom of southern [[Phiweriyu]], a land of marshes, black water, raised timber roads, reed-choked channels, and settlements that survive by adaptation rather than mastery. Outsiders often dismiss it as a damp and decaying backwater, but that judgment misses the harder truth of Fennock: it is a kingdom that has endured precisely because its people know how to live where cleaner and prouder realms would fail. What the land lacks in stability, it repays in concealment, strange abundance, and stubborn resilience. Rare herbs, swamp medicines, alchemical reagents, peat, fish, and hidden trade routes all make Fennock more valuable than its reputation suggests.

The kingdom’s defining crisis is that its capital is slowly sinking into the marsh. Foundations fail, old pilings rot, stone lists, and each season threatens to claim another district unless labor crews, engineers, and imported materials are constantly put to work. This disaster is not a single collapse but an ongoing condition of political life. It drains coin, divides the nobility, and forces the crown to think in terms of repair, salvage, and survival rather than splendor. The result is a monarchy that governs under pressure and a court where every public plan eventually becomes an argument about cost, urgency, and who should bear the burden.

That vulnerability has made Fennock deeply entangled with its neighbors. The kingdom relies on lumber from [[Thiadery]] to reinforce failing foundations, while routes and financial support through [[Larnack]] remain essential to moving crews, contracts, and material. Lords such as [[Faelkir Hold]] thrive in this environment because control of a causeway or dike can become as politically important as any noble title. Within Fennock itself, houses like [[House Morcant]] profit from their command of marsh passages and salvage economies, while houses such as [[House Dunlaith]] stake their future on keeping the capital standing long enough for the realm to remain a coherent whole.

This has given Fennock a political culture unlike any of the other kingdoms. Rank matters, but geography often matters more. A noble with a sound embankment, usable labor crews, and reliable ferries may hold more real power than one with impeccable blood and no way to move men through flood season. Courtly language still exists, but it is often stripped down by necessity. Fennocki politics are full of bargains made in crisis, obligations carried over from storms and sinkings, and rivalries sharpened by the knowledge that every disaster produces both loss and opportunity.

Culturally, Fennock is defined by endurance, suspicion, and a rough familiarity with decay. Its people build high, move often, and trust those who understand the marsh more than those who merely speak well in halls. This gives the kingdom a reputation for smugglers, salvagers, and half-lawful enterprise, much of which is deserved. Yet beneath that reputation is a society that has become adept at turning instability into a way of life. Fennock survives because it has had no choice but to do so.

For adventurers, Fennock offers a setting of drowned ruins, unstable districts, hidden channels, labor unrest, smuggling routes, and crises that can turn local fast. A shipment of lumber, the collapse of an embankment, or the disappearance of a marsh guide can matter as much here as a lost relic or royal decree elsewhere. In Fennock, catastrophe is never far away, and neither is the chance for someone ambitious to profit from it.
