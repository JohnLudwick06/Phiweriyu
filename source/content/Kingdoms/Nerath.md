#Kingdom 
Nerath is the northeastern kingdom of [[Phiweriyu]], and home to the oldest continuously inhabited city on the mainland. More than any of the other realms, Nerath lives in the shadow of the [[Old Empire]] and deliberately cultivates that inheritance. Its priests, magistrates, and scholars maintain long archives, ancient rites, and civic traditions that predate the fall of the imperial capital. To the Nerathi mind, age is not merely a curiosity but a form of legitimacy, and the kingdom’s elites often speak as though history itself sits in judgment over the younger powers around them.

Geographically, Nerath is shaped by its long coast, inland routes, and access to the shattered archipelagos offshore. Though not as commercially central as [[Larnack]], it commands old sea lanes, fortified harbors, and maritime approaches whose strategic value is difficult to ignore. Inland, older roads tie temple towns, estates, and administrative centers back toward the ancient city. Nerath’s land is not defined by lavish abundance so much as by continuity and control. It is a kingdom that prefers old stones, remembered obligations, and laws that have outlived dynasties.

Politically, Nerath draws much of its confidence from this sense of inheritance. Its crown rules through a mixture of ceremony, religion, and civic tradition, arbitrating among noble houses that all imagine themselves protectors of a greater legacy. Noble authority in Nerath tends to divide between houses rooted in the old city and its institutions, and those whose power comes from ships, ports, and control of the coast. [[House Aedraig]] embodies the first tradition, grounding its influence in ritual, law, and the careful stewardship of memory. [[House Rhuvann]] represents the second, rising through harbors, patrols, and maritime enforcement. These two strains are often in tension, but both agree that Nerath must act as a serious kingdom rather than a relic living on stories.

That conviction is at the heart of Nerath’s feud with [[Thiadery]]. Nerath seized one of Thiadery’s islands, and the act has become far more than a territorial dispute. To yield the island now would be to admit weakness, surrender prestige, and undercut the kingdom’s self-image as the disciplined heir of an older order. Many Nerathi nobles therefore speak of the island not in terms of opportunism but of rightful firmness. The matter has only intensified since [[Fuarnarth]] shifted its food purchases from Thiadery to Nerath, bringing additional wealth and leverage to the kingdom. That trade has emboldened those who believe Nerath can press its advantage without yet crossing into outright war.

Even so, Nerath is not a kingdom of simple belligerence. Its rulers know that the same historical pride that gives the realm strength can also harden into inflexibility. The kings seek peace, but theirs is the peace of dignified advantage, not meek compromise. Every treaty is weighed against precedent. Every slight is remembered longer than outsiders expect. Every alliance is judged not only for usefulness but for what it implies about rank, inheritance, and who truly speaks with the weight of history.

To travelers, Nerath can seem austere, formal, and quietly contemptuous of improvisation. Its courts are full of manners sharpened into weapons, and its temples preserve memories that may be politically inconvenient in the present but dangerous to ignore. Yet its sternness is part of what has allowed it to endure. Nerath remains a kingdom where law matters, archives matter, and the past is not dead background but a living force in current affairs. For adventurers, it is a land of old claims, sea-borne rivalries, sacred precedent, and nobles who can turn a question of honor into a crisis between realms.
