#Kingdom
Thiadery is one of the most fertile kingdoms in [[Phiweriyu]], a broad and productive land of farms, river valleys, managed forests, and dispersed lordships. Where [[Larnack]] draws wealth from roads and tolls, Thiadery draws power from what the land itself can produce. Grain, livestock, timber, and labor all move outward from Thiadery into the wider continent, making it indispensable even when its rulers struggle to turn abundance into unified strength. Much of the realm’s influence is therefore indirect: it feeds its neighbors, shores up their failing structures, and supplies raw material to powers that often underestimate how much they depend on it.

This agricultural wealth is paired with a political structure that is notably less centralized than many of its rivals. Thiadery is a kingdom of strong regions, local obligations, and great families whose power rests on productive land rather than singular courtly authority. The crown rules, but it does so through negotiation as much as decree. River lords, timber houses, grain magnates, and provincial stewards all expect to be consulted, rewarded, and accommodated. This gives Thiadery depth and resilience, but it can also make the kingdom appear hesitant at moments when its enemies are more unified or simply more willing to act.

That tension defines much of Thiadery’s present political life. The kingdom is feuding with [[Nerath]] over an island Nerath has taken, and the loss is as humiliating as it is strategic. Many Thiaderyn nobles believe the insult became possible only because the realm’s internal divisions made decisive response too slow. Others argue that war over a single island would be ruinous unless the kingdom first learns to coordinate its own strength. These arguments have grown more bitter since [[Fuarnarth]] shifted its grain purchases away from Thiadery and toward Nerath, rewarding Thiadery’s rival at precisely the moment the kingdom most needed to project steadiness.

Thiadery’s significance, however, has not diminished. If anything, its resources have become even more politically consequential. [[Fennock]] now depends on Thiadery lumber to reinforce the foundations of its sinking capital, and that demand has turned forest routes, barges, and timber contracts into matters of interstate importance. Houses like [[House Tiernan]] influence the politics of food, storage, and regional bargaining, while houses such as [[House Brennath]] shape not only forestry and labor but the fate of distant cities that would struggle to stand without Thiadery wood. In this sense, Thiadery exports both stability and leverage, whether it intends to or not.

Culturally, Thiadery feels more grounded and local than kingdoms built on old imperial identity or harsh environmental engineering. Its loyalties often begin with estate, river, parish, and harvest before they extend to abstract royal policy. That can make the kingdom seem less glamorous to outsiders, but it also gives it a kind of rooted endurance. Thiadery is not a realm that impresses by ceremony alone. It impresses by the quiet truth that much of the continent continues to eat, build, and trade because Thiadery’s fields and forests keep producing.

For adventurers, Thiadery is a land of convoy politics, harvest tensions, river disputes, local feuds, and great houses whose practical concerns can have continental consequences. A grain levy, a timber delay, or a feud between landed families may matter as much to the future of peace as any diplomatic summit. The kingdom’s greatest danger is not weakness of resources, but weakness of cohesion. If Thiadery ever learns to wield its abundance with a single purpose, the balance of the whole continent may shift.
