# Larnack

Larnack is a central crossroads kingdom occupying the broad plains at the heart of [[Phiweriyu]]. Its soil is only modestly fertile, but a dense web of roads and rivers turns it into the primary conduit of commerce between the surrounding realms. Caravans from [[Nerath]], barges from [[Thiadery]], timber trains out of [[Fennock]], and ore caravans bound for or from [[Fuarnarth]] all pass through Larnack’s toll gates and warehouses. As a result, Larnack’s wealth comes less from harvests and more from tariffs, banking, and tradecraft—counting houses and merchant guilds are as influential here as old noble bloodlines.

Though it sits at the center of the map, Larnack is not an uncontested hegemon. Instead, it is a kingdom under constant diplomatic pressure, pulled in several directions by its neighbors. Nerath, the ancient northeastern city-realm, prides itself on imperial tradition and resents Larnack’s commercial dominance. Thiadery, richer in farmland and forests, sees Larnack as its essential outlet to distant markets. Fennock’s swampy lowlands rely on Larnack’s engineers and financiers to keep their sinking city above the waterline. Fuarnarth, far to the cold south with its volcanic warrens and forges, needs Larnack’s roads to move metal, weapons, and mercenaries northward. In practice, Larnack functions as the hinge on which interstate politics turns, even when its rulers would prefer the kingdom to be only a neutral marketplace.

Internally, Larnack is divided into a patchwork of provinces, each dominated by entrenched noble houses with their own foreign loyalties. The crown officially pursues peace and balanced relations with all kingdoms, but the reality in court is a subtle cold war fought through coin, contracts, and couriers. [[House Brannoc]], from the northern crossroads, favors Nerath and drapes itself in half-remembered imperial customs. [[Clan Eirsvold]], based in the mineral hills and along the road to Fuarnarth, champions armories, fortifications, and closer military ties with southern smiths and engineers. [[House Caerwyn]], nestled beside the great eastern river junction, profits from Thiadery’s grain and timber, quietly encouraging dependence on food that passes through its granaries. Jarl [[Faelkir]], ruling from the dike-town that guards the causeway into Fennock, enriches himself by controlling the flow of lumber and loans into that failing marsh-realm.

Because each of these powers speaks with a different external voice, Larnack’s politics are perpetually fractious. The same policy—a grain treaty, a road levy, an offer of joint maneuvers—can mean advantage for one faction and ruin for another. Court sessions in Larnack are therefore less about clear ideological divides and more about competing trade interests, dressed up as arguments over tradition, security, or mercy. The king’s council spends as much time preventing his own nobles from dragging the kingdom into somebody else’s war as it does managing affairs of state.

For travelers and adventurers, Larnack is both opportunity and danger. Work is plentiful; merchants need guards, lords need deniable agents, and every provincial magnate has a rival they would like quietly embarrassed or exposed. At the same time, taking coin from one patron can implicitly place a party in a wider geopolitical camp without their realizing it. A simple caravan escort might, in hindsight, prove to have been the crucial grain shipment that let one kingdom outlast another in a blockade, or the weapons convoy that turned a border skirmish into a crisis. As tensions between the realms slowly rise, Larnack’s cities and waystations become listening posts for rumors of disputes over islands, shifting grain contracts, and sinking foundations in distant swamps.

Culturally, Larnack reflects its role as a meeting ground. Its markets and taverns host Nerathi scholars, Thiadery farmers, Fennock marsh-folk, and Fuarnarth engineers rubbing shoulders with native Larnackers. Dialects mix, fashions shift with trade winds, and religious practice incorporates shrines to foreign saints and local spirits alike. To outsiders, Larnack can appear rootless and mercantile; to its own people, the kingdom is the living proof that different lands can coexist so long as coin and carts keep moving. Whether that fragile belief survives the coming age of heroes and would-be redeemers is one of the central questions hanging over the realm.
